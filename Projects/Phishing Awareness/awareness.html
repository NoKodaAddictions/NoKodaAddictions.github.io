If You Were Given A Link via Email

You Have Just Fallen For A Phishing Attack!

What Is A Phishing Attack?

A Phishing Attack is a branch of social engineering. Just like other attacks,
Phishing is a way for a hacker or scammer to get access to passwords,
contacts, phone calls, etc. Your information could then be sold on the dark web
for huge amounts of money.

How Do Phishing Attacks Work?

As stated above, the attacker would ususally send a text or email with a
link to a malicious website. But, the attacker wouldn't just send a plain old email,
oh no no no. The attacker will try to find some information about you. Through your
public social media profile, posts, etc.

Example

From: Twitter Team

Subject: Account Problems

Hello Linda.

Our team has detected malicious actions from your @LindaOfficial account
these actions may cause your account to be banned. Please go to this
page to see the report of the complaints. [insert malicious link here]

*Linda is just a dummy character

How Can You Prevent Yourself?

Make sure you analyze who the sender is. Gmail and Outlook have features
to check who the sender is (Click the arrow button next to the 'to me' text).

In the image, the sender is from Google. Although they do provide
A link to the webpage, if you are skeptical about it, open a new tab and type in
the link to the page.

Other Types Of Attacks

1. Whaling - When A Hacker Goes After A Person At The Top Of The Work Chain
2. Smishing and Vishing - Hackers Send Messages Via Text Or Telephone

Extensions and Antivirus Software

If you want to take an extra step to keep yourself safe, install extensions.
I reccomend installing uBlock Origin to block ads and some malicious websites.
For antivirus software, I reccomend McAfee. They include the software to block
viruses and malware, and the extensions to prevent from downloading them.

Another thing you can do is enable 2-factor authentication. Google And Microsoft
have the feature for it. Just go to your 'profile settings' and then click 'security'.

Conclusion

As many people switiching to online work, school, etc, it gives hackers a better
chance of stealing private information. Many school teachers, and employees
usually recive dozens of emails a day, and they may not realize 1 of their emails
may be malicious. Whenever you read an email that you are skeptical about
(refer to Figure 1), take the steps above to keep yourself safe.
